How to use it

10 minutes a day is all it takes

Short, consistent sessions work better than long ones.

1

Pick up where you left off

The built-in progress tracker means no planning needed.

2

Do 2–3 pages together

Read the instruction, let your child try first before helping.

3

Check the difficulty dots

Each page shows how hard it is — stop when it gets too challenging.

4

Repeat tricky pages

It's fine to redo pages. Repetition is how skills actually stick.
